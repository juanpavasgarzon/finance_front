# Finance — User Guide

## Getting Started

Finance is a personal finance management application that helps you track income, expenses, and plan your monthly budget.

### Registration & Login

1. Visit the application URL
2. Click **Register** to create a new account with email and password
3. After registration, log in with your credentials
4. You will be redirected to the Dashboard

### Language & Theme

- Use the **ES/EN** toggle to switch between Spanish and English
- Use the **Light/Dark** toggle to switch themes
- The language is reflected in the URL (`/es/...` or `/en/...`)

---

## Dashboard

The Dashboard provides an overview of your financial activity for a selected period.

- **Period selector**: Choose Weekly, Monthly, or Yearly
- **Date picker**: Select the reference date
- **Summary cards**: Total income, total expenses, and balance
- **Overview chart**: Horizontal bar chart comparing income, expenses, and balance
- **Pie charts**: Visual breakdown of expenses and income by category
- **Download Excel**: Export the dashboard data to an Excel file

---

## Planner

The Financial Planner helps you simulate your payroll and monthly budget. All data is stored locally in your browser.

### Payroll Tab

Configure your salary and deductions:

- **Base Income**: Your monthly gross salary
- **Transport Aid**: Enable and set the amount if your employer provides it
- **Health Deduction**: Monthly health insurance amount deducted from your salary
- **Pension Deduction**: Monthly pension contribution amount
- **FSP**: Solidarity Pension Fund contribution (if applicable)
- **Loan**: Configure payroll loan deductions with total amount, interest rate, and dates
- **Other Deductions**: Tax withholding and miscellaneous deductions
- **Pay Stub**: Preview your biweekly pay stub with all earnings and deductions
- **Monthly Cash Flow**: See your net income minus fixed expenses

### Extras Tab

Record irregular or one-time income:

- Enter the date, source description, and amount
- View history of all extra income entries
- Delete entries as needed

### Fixed Expenses Tab

Manage recurring monthly expenses:

- Add expenses with concept name, amount, and biweekly assignment (1Q or 2Q)
- See the monthly total at the bottom

---

## Summary

The Summary page shows a 12-month financial projection based on your planner configuration.

- **Year selector**: View projections for different years
- **Annual totals**: Income, expenses, and balance for the selected year
- **Monthly table**: Payroll, extras, total income, expenses, balance, and accumulated savings
- **Download CSV**: Export the summary data

---

## Categories

Organize your expenses and income into categories.

- Click **Create category** to add a new one
- Select the type: Expense or Income
- Categories are displayed as chips grouped by type

---

## Schedules

Automate recurring expenses and income.

- **Create schedule**: Define name, amount, type, category, recurrence interval, and next due date
- Schedules automatically generate expenses or income entries on their due dates
- Generated entries start as unpaid/pending

---

## Expenses

Track all your expenses.

- **Add expense**: Create a new expense with name, amount, category, description, and due date
- Manually created expenses are automatically marked as paid
- Schedule-generated expenses appear as **Unpaid** — click **Pay** to mark them as paid
- View all expenses in a table sorted by date

---

## Incomes

Track all your income.

- **Add income**: Create a new income entry with name, amount, category, description, and due date
- Manually created incomes are automatically marked as received
- Schedule-generated incomes appear as **Pending** — click **Receive** to mark them as received

---

## Notifications

The notification bell in the top-right corner shows real-time alerts about:

- Upcoming schedule due dates
- New generated expenses/income from schedules

---

## Search

Use the search bar in the header to quickly navigate to any page in the application.

---

## Tips

- Use the **Planner** to simulate different salary scenarios before committing
- Create **Categories** first, then use them in expenses, incomes, and schedules
- Set up **Schedules** for recurring bills to never miss a payment
- Check the **Summary** page monthly to track your financial progress
- Export data regularly using the download buttons for record keeping
